Food & Wine

Our itinerary is part of a school project aimed at discovering and showcasing, in our own way, the city of Rome through its flavors and an unforgettable experience. The itinerary includes a selection of traditional Roman eateries for breakfast, lunch, and dinner, with recommended dishes tailored to different preferences (meat, fish, vegetarian, etc.), the ideal wine or beverage pairing, and an approximate indication of the costs. Additionally, there will be some “bonus” spots where you can enjoy small snacks, perfect for recharging after hours of walking through the heart of Rome.

by Simone

1° stop
Roscioli Caffè (1-10€)

Roscioli Caffè - Located at Piazza Benedetto Cairoli 16, in the heart of Rome’s historic center, it is an elegant café and pastry shop run by the renowned Roscioli family, active for four generations.

Hours: 7:00-18:00 (Sunday from 8:00 AM)

Address: Piazza Benedetto Cairoli, 16

La Casa del Caffè Tazza d'Oro (1-10€)

Tazza d'Oro - Founded in 1946, just a few steps from the Pantheon. Famous for its blend “La Regina dei Caffè” and its coffee granita with whipped cream.

Hours: 7:00-20:00

Address: Via degli Orfani, 84

Transfer: 15-30 min on foot

2° stop
Trattoria da Teo - Trastevere (20-30€)

A welcoming atmosphere with outdoor seating available, ideal for enjoying Roman flavors in a charming setting.

Recommended dishes: Carbonara, Amatriciana, Cacio e pepe, Carciofo alla giudia

Wine pairing: Frascati Superiore DOCG

Address: Piazza dei Ponziani, 7/A

Trapizzino - Trastevere (5-10€)

A brilliant Roman invention that combines pizza and bread, creating a triangle of leavened dough filled with the classics of Roman cuisine.

Specialties: Pollo alla cacciatora, Polpetta al sugo, Parmigiana di melanzane

Wine pairing: Cesanese del Piglio DOCG

Address: Piazza Trilussa, 46

Transfer: 10-20 min on foot

3° stop
Supplizio

A gourmet fry shop created by chef Arcangelo Dandini, renowned for his passion for supplì and traditional Roman cuisine.

Specialties: Supplì classico, alla carbonara, cacio e pepe

Address: Via dei Banchi Vecchi, 143

Gelateria Giolitti

Founded in 1890, it is one of the historic gelaterias in Rome, frequented by locals, tourists, and celebrities for generations.

Specialties: Nocciola, pistacchio, cioccolato, champagne, fichi

Address: Via degli Uffici del Vicario, 40

Transfer: 15-30 min on foot

Ristorante Jole (25-30€)

Ristorante Jole - A hidden gem in the heart of Testaccio, paying tribute to the cuisine of Roman grandmothers with a touch of creativity.

Recommended dishes: Ravioli di coda alla vaccinara, Bombolotti alla carbonara, Bollito alla picchiapò

Address: Piazza Orazio Giustiniani, 2

Phone number: +39 06 574 1382

Er Polpetta dal 1987 (25-30€)

Known for its traditional Roman cuisine, with a particular focus on meatballs, from which it takes its name.

Recommended dishes: Polpette di manzo alla romana, Fettuccine al ragù, Amatriciana, Ossobuco

Address: Via di Tor Millina, 24

Hours: 11:00-23:00
