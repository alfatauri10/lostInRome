Castelli Romani

Our itinerary was created as part of a school project aimed at discovering and promoting the historical and natural heritage of the Lazio region. We decided to explore the Castelli Romani area, a place full of charming villages, historical villas, volcanic landscapes, and local food traditions. The journey includes six must-see stops: Frascati, Villa Aldobrandini, Grottaferrata, Nemi, Castel Gandolfo, and Lake Albano. Each stop has been enriched with historical research, practical tips, and local secrets, gathered and shared by us students, with the goal of offering an authentic and personal experience of the area.

by Matteo

2
Frascati e Villa Aldobrandini

Elegant historical buildings and the irresistible aroma of freshly baked bread.

▼

Informations

Position: Frascati, Piazza Marconi
Transport from Rome: €2.10 (regional train)
Villa opening hours: 9:00-13:00 / 15:00-18:00
Advice: Arrive early to enjoy the view of Rome from the terrace of Villa Aldobrandini with the best light for photos!
1° stage

Start your day by going up to the beautiful Villa Aldobrandini, which overlooks the town with its amazing gardens. After a walk around the historic center, checking out wine shops and craft stores, it’s the perfect time for a tasting.

Typical tasting: Simonetti Winery

Simonetti Winery - Where the wine tells the story of the land. Under the cobblestone streets of Frascati, there’s a hidden world of oak barrels and the smell of fresh wine waiting for you. A glass of Frascati DOCG, some local snacks, and the story of an old tradition make this experience super authentic and cool.

Opening hours: 10:00-19:00

Cost: €10-15 each

Continuation: 7 minutes by car or 15 minutes by Cotral bus (€1.50).
3
Grottaferrata and the Abbey of San Nilo.

Where time seems to have stopped in 1004.

▼

Informations

Foundation: Year 1004
Entry: Free entry
Opening hours: 8:00-12:30 / 15:30-19:00
Curiosity: The name "Grottaferrata" comes from a crypt protected by an iron grate, visible under the altar.
2° stage

The Abbey of San Nilo will welcome you with its mystical atmosphere and precious frescoes. If you're lucky, you might get to visit it accompanied by one of the Basilian monks.

Quick lunch: Forneria Ceralli

Hot and crispy porchetta sandwich + drink €7. A tasty and informal break, perfect to enjoy on a bench in front of the Abbey, just like the locals do.

Opening hours: 7:00-20:00

Continuation: 25 minutes by car or Cotral bus (€1.50).
4
Nemi and the Roman Ship Museum.

A village that looks like it's straight out of a fairy tale, overlooking the volcanic lake.

▼

Informations

Roman Ships Museum: Caligula's boats.
Museum entry: €5
Museum opening hours: 9:00-19:00
Event: Don't miss. The Strawberry Festival. (held in June), with stalls, costume parades, and live music.
3° stage

Walk through flower-filled balconies and take a look at the belvedere to admire the volcanic lake. Just a little further down, the Roman Ships Museum houses the impressive boats of Caligula.

Snack: Il Chiosco della Nonna

Il Chiosco della Nonna - Sweetness with a view. Fresh strawberries, whipped cream, and a breathtaking view of the lake: a moment of pure pleasure not to be missed.

Opening hours: 10:00-19:00

Cost: €4-6

Continuation: 15 minutes by car or Cotral bus (€1.50).
5
Castel Gandolfo

The ancient summer residence of the Popes, overlooking Lake Albano.

▼

Informations

Papal Palace: Papal summer residence
Garden entry: €10
Garden opening hours: 9:00-18:00
View: From the terrace of the Barberini Gardens, you can enjoy a breathtaking view of the lake.
4° stage

The ancient summer residence of the Popes, this gem overlooking the lake will surprise you. By visiting the Papal Palace and the Barberini Gardens, you'll discover an intimate and green side of Rome.

Drinks before dinner: Art and wine

Art and wine - Authentic flavors at sunset. Seasonal cocktails, local cured meats, and a lovely terrace: the aperitivo here becomes a slow ritual, between nature and beauty.

Opening hours 17:00-23:00

Cost: €10-15

Continuation: Walk down (10 minutes)
6
Lake Albano

Clear waters, silence, and unspoiled nature.

▼

Informations

Pedal boat: Available at the kiosks.
Pedal boat rental. €10/h
Beaches: Public and equipped
Pic-nic: Recommended with coppiette and Frascati bread!
5° stage

Here you can relax, take a swim, or rent a pedal boat to explore the coast from a different perspective. The sunset over the lake is a must-see.

Optional dinner: Quintessa Restaurant

Quintessa Restaurant - For those who want to indulge in style. Refined cuisine with local products and a lake view, the perfect place to end the day.

Opening hours: 19:30-23:00

Cost: €30-50 a persona
