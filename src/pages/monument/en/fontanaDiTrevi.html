Fontana di Trevi

The Trevi Fountain, eternal jewel of Rome and triumph of Baroque artistry.

History and construction

This monument was commissioned by Pope Clement XII, and consequently by a competition won by the architect Nicola Salvi. He followed the project until his death, 10 years before the construction's completion, in 1762. The fountain represents the union between Baroque architecture and sculpture, with the figure of Oceanus at the center, sitting on a shell-shaped chariot pulled by seahorses.

How it is used

Nowadays, this fountain is one of the most visited monuments in Rome, attracting millions of tourists each year, who follow the tradition of throwing a coin in, believing that it could secure them a happy return to the "eternal city".

Recent facts

In 2015, Fendi sponsored this landmark, which was restored with an investment of 22 million euros.

On October 4th 2024, this fountain underwent important cleaning and maintenance, and during the work, a footbridge was installed.

This monument owes its fame to Federico Fellini and other filmmakers, who filmed many movies here.

Legends and fun facts

  • Name: Its name originates from the ancient Latin word "trivium", meaning the merging of three streets leading to the square where the fountain is located.
  • Coins: The tradition of throwing a coin into the fountain with your right hand over your left shoulder comes from a legend that says this gesture would guarantee a future return to Rome.
  • Virgin Water: The fountain is powered by the Acqua Vergine aqueduct, one of the few Roman aqueducts still in operation, built in 19 B.C. by Marco Vispasiano Agrippa.
