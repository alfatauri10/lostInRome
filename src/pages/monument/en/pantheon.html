Pantheon

The Pantheon, timeless symbol of Rome and marvel of ancient Roman architecture

History and construction

The first Pantheon was built in 27 b.C. by Marco Vipsanio Agrippa, a Roman general and friend of Emperor Augusto. However, this first building was destroyed by fire. The current Pantheon was rebuilt by Emperor Adriano around 118-125 b.C.

It was initially built as a temple for all the Roman gods. Later, in 609 b.C. , it became a Christian church, which helped preserve it in good condition.

How it is used

The Pantheon has been used as a Catholic church called the Basilica di Santa Maria degli Angeli e dei Martiri since 609 b.C. Religious ceremonies are still held there today.

Millions of visitors come to see the Pantheon every year. People are amazed by its large dome and the oculus, which lets in natural light.

Recent facts

The Pantheon was included in the UNESCO World Heritage Site list in 1980 and later expanded in 1990. This highlights its historical, artistic, and cultural importance.

This recognition helps protect and preserve the monument, ensuring that future generations can continue to admire and study this extraordinary example of Roman engineering and architecture.

Legends and fun facts

  • The Pantheon has survived many earthquakes, but it has not been completely unscathed. The building has suffered some damage in the past, most notably in the 9th century.
  • The Pantheon has appeared in many famous films. For example, in the 2009 movie Angels & Demons and in the 2015 film The Man from U.N.C.L.E.
  • It is said that during certain times of the year, the sunlight shines through the oculus in a special way and highlights the statues or altars inside, making it look like the gods are blessing the temple.
  • The floor of the Pantheon is made of colored marble from different parts of the Roman Empire.
